Purpose

The world is in the middle of a polycrisis. Climate change, biodiversity loss, social polarisation and geopolitical tensions reinforce one another. Most existing responses remain disciplinary, technical or short-term.

We do not lack information. We lack leadership that understands complexity, accepts responsibility, and decides from a broader ethical and intellectual ground.

This is precisely where the ThinkLink Association begins.

Three paths

We connect what is too often thought apart.

i.

Transdisciplinarity beyond academic silos

Scientific findings are brought together with practical experience and with philosophical and cultural perspectives. We create spaces in which disciplines illuminate one another rather than exclude each other.

ii.

Education and responsible leadership

Capability extends beyond expertise. Orientation, judgement and a sense of responsibility are cultivated deliberately. Education becomes a place to test what works in business, politics and society.

iii.

A vertical, spiritual dimension of thought

Older wisdom traditions and philosophical schools open an additional plane of insight. Reflection and attentiveness extend intuition and ethical sensitivity beyond purely analytical categories.

Book series

An international edition with Springer Nature.

The book series Transdisciplinary Management of Ecological and Social Crises gathers international voices from science, practice and philosophy. It is edited by Prof. Dr. Mathias Schüz and Prof. Dr. René Schmidpeter and published by Springer Nature.

Members also publish regularly in textbooks, edited collections and journals, including the Journal of Sustainable Business.

Partnership & support

A commitment measured in generations, not quarters.

We seek conversation with foundations, family offices and companies that understand transdisciplinary research, ethical education and responsible leadership as long-term strategic concerns.

The ThinkLink Association is a non-profit association registered in the Canton of Zürich and exempt from taxation. It supports institutional research, the book series at Springer Nature, international symposia and the development of the SEED Academy as the future educational arm of the association.

A partnership with ThinkLink is deliberately not a transaction. Engagements are shaped in personal conversation, adapted to each partner's concerns and time horizon, and reported in the annual statement of accounts.

The board is available for an initial, confidential conversation.

Legal form Non-profit association under Swiss law, Art. 60 Civil Code. Registered in the Canton of Zürich.
Tax status Exempt from taxation. Contributions are tax-deductible in Switzerland.
Governance Three board members, annual members' meeting, audit per the statutes.
